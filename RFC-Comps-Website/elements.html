Overview

Throughout Fall 2018 and Winter 2019, our team worked with the Carleton campus student group, the Real Food Calculator, to improve their workflow. Twice a year they record all of Bon Appetit’s purchases and calculate the percentage of items in the Carleton dining hall that are real as defined by the national Real Food Challenge. Ultimately, we aimed to help them more efficiently and effectively meet their goal of shifting institutional spending from conventional to “real” food. After informally interviewing them about their process and observing their work sessions, we decided to create a website and database system to help ease the process of data entry and to visualize food purchasing trends. On the data entry portion of our website, we improved their data entry process by reducing redundant researching and typing. On the visualization portion of our website, we have provided graphical representations that allow RFC volunteers to identify which specific products are most beneficial to shift from conventional to real.

Originally, the data entry process involved manually entering receipt information into a spreadsheet and digging through previous spreadsheets to look up items, which is time consuming. The data entry portion of our website mimics their old workflow by allowing one user to begin an entry session, type all items for a particular receipt, double check and submit them to the database. Major improvements include allowing a user to copy and update fields of old entries on the same page they insert

We decided to reduce the amount of time it takes to enter receipts into a document by creating a data entry section in our website. This section contains an entry session page that shows all items that have been entered from the current receipt as well as a data entry page with allows the user to enter new items into the entry session. The data entry page auto populates fields based on previous entries into the database so that the user can type less and spend less time researching. The RFC group told us that once the they has entered all of the receipts for a time period, they want to have the ability to know the best ways to increase the percentage of real food served at Carleton. As a result, we created a visualization page on our website. The page contains a copious amount of graphs, pie charts, and tables that help the user understand what the big picture of the data is. For example, there is a bar chart of the top 5 non-real foods by dollars purchased. This graph is a great place to find items that need extra focus by the RFC group. Our website should allow the RFC group to spend more time and effort on increasing the total real food percentage at Carleton and less time entering data.

Project Goals:

Architecture:

  • Centralized and organized database system with past data imported to it.
  • A user interface that is easier and more organized to use than excel that inputs information into a centralized database system.
  • An error correcting system incorporated within the user interface.
  • A software that analyzes given data and spits out visual representations of the data.
  • Together, goal was to improve workflow by cutting time spent on data clean-up, shortening the wait on getting back results, eradicating the need to manually input repetitive data, and making past data more accessible/usable.

Data Entry Needs:

  • Reduce amount of unnecessary typing by using past data to intelligently inform current data entry.
  • Ability to recognize a product that has been entered before based upon product code, description, vendor, etc.
  • Ability to replicate a previously-entered product and change the cost.
  • Ease of entering particular fields (cost, description, product code) of a product, for example, movement through arrows v. clicking.
  • Ability to accept only valid entries for a particular data field and ensure correctness of data.
  • Ability to download data in correct format to send to the national RFC
  • Data adheres to national RFC guidelines
